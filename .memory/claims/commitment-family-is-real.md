# Goal, Constraint, Decision, And Alternative Are One Will-Apt Family

Goal, Constraint, Decision, and Alternative are all *will-apt* directives that
share a common structure: a **stance applied to a proposition**.

- Goal = *achieve* a proposition (make it true, often by a deadline)
- Constraint = *maintain* a proposition (keep it from becoming false)
- Decision = *select* a proposition (commit to an option)
- Alternative = *propose* a proposition (a candidate commitment under consideration)

They are the conative dual of [[proposition-is-durable-unit|Claim]]: where a Claim
is a proposition moved by evidence, a directive is a proposition moved by
deliberation.

## Evidence

- The Alternative → Decision lifecycle (`proposed → accepted`) mirrors a Claim's
  `unsettled → settled`. When Question was removed, Alternatives re-parented onto
  the open Decision — the will-apt side already behaves like one lifecycle.
- Each named primitive reduces to `stance × proposition`, differing only in the
  stance (achieve / maintain / select / propose).

This Claim is settled. Whether to *unify* the family into one primitive is a
separate, open matter — see the decision it grounds.
